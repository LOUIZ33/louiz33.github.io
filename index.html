Noodling Around with Flavour: Bolognese Done Right

Author: Louise Z

Posted: 15th September 2024

  • 15 minutes

  • 60-90 minutes

  • 4 people

This spaghetti bolognese recipe is a hearty, classic Italian dish that's perfect for family dinners or meal prepping. With rich, slow-cooked flavours and a delicious combination of meat and tomato, this comforting recipe has been a household favourite for years. Serve it with fresh salad and crusty bread for a truly satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • Olive Oil - 2 tablespoons
  • Onion - 1 large, finely chopped
  • Garlic - 3 cloves, minced
  • Ground beef - 500g
  • Tomato paste - 2 tablespoons
  • Canned tomatoes - 400g
  • Beef stock - 1/2 cup
  • Red wine (optional) - 1/2 cup
  • Bay leaf - 1 or 2 leaves
  • Dried Oregano - 1 teaspoon
  • Dried basil - 1 teaspoon
  • Salt and pepper - to taste
  • Spaghetti - 40g
  • Parmesan cheese - grated, for serving

Instructions

  1. Prepare the vegetables. Finely chop the onion and garlic
  2. Saute the veegtables. Heat the oliveoil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the onion for about 3-5 minutes, until softened. Stir the garlic and cook for another minute until fragnant.
  3. Brown the ground beef. Add the ground beef to the pan, breaking it up with a spoon. Cook for about 8-10 minutes until browned all over
  4. Add tomato paste and deglaze with red wine. Stor the tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes. If using, pour the red wine and let it simmer for 3-5 minutes until reduced slightly.
  5. Add tomato and seasoning. Add the canned tomatoes, beef stock, bay leaf, oregano, and basil. Stir well season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Simmer the sauce. Lower the head and let it simmer gently for 45 minutes, stirring ocassionally. This helps develop rich flavours. Add water or stock if it becomes too thick
  7. Cook the spaghetti. About 10 minutes before the sauce is done, sook the spaghetti in a pot of boilin salted water according to package intructions until al dente. Drain the spaghetti, reserving a little pasta consistency if needed.
  8. Combine and serve. Remove the bay leaf from sauce. Toss the cooked spaghetti with the sauce, adding some pasta water if necessary to loosen the sauce. Serve hot, topped with grated Parmesan cheese.
